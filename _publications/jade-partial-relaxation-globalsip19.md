This paper applies a partial-relaxation strategy to the joint angle and delay estimation (JADE) problem in multipath wireless channels. By selectively relaxing the joint maximum-likelihood objective, the proposed estimator achieves computational efficiency without sacrificing estimation accuracy relative to fully joint approaches.
